Global comparative study of bacterial meningitis seasonality

Global Comparative Study of Bacterial Meningitis Seasonality

L'Institut de Recherche pour le Développement (IRD) - Montpellier, France

I'm currently collaborating with Dr. Hélène Broutin from L'Institut de Recherche pour le Développement (IRD) in Montpellier, France and Dr. Nicole Basta from Princeton University on a global comparative study of the dynamics of bacterial meningitis. The goal of my project is to compile a global database of bacterial meningitis incidence time series by scraping public health ministry websites/reports (in addition to manual correspondence) and to analyze the seasonality of this data via wavelet analysis, particularly in countries outside of the African meningitis belt. Whereas distinct seasonality patterns have been thoroughly studied in the African meningitis belt, little is known about its dynamics in other regions of the world, making it difficult to tailor vaccination strategies and effectively control the disease. A stronger understanding of the biological and environmental interactions of the various causative agents of meningitis will help us better understand its virulence and epidemiology.


Reasoning from Radically Incomplete Information

Reasoning from Radically Incomplete Information: The Case of Containers

The Courant Institute of Mathematical Sciences - New York, NY

During my internship at NYU's Courant Institute of Mathematical Science, I researched and published a paper about "common sense logic," wherein humans arrive at logical conclusions despite extremely sparse available information, thereby becoming both more efficient and accurate than computers in information-sparse environments. I programmed a system of mathematical axioms describing first-order spatiotemporal container logic and proved related theorems in SPASS, demonstrating the first steps towards precisely describing the human physical inference process.

My Web Development Work

Princeton Model United Nations Conference

I'm currently working on the website for the Princeton Model United Nations Conference (PMUNC), which includes not only a front-end designed with Bootstrap, SmoothScroll, HTML/CSS, Javascript, and AJAX but also a back-end PHP/MySQL system that seamlessly integrates conference registration with application submission, country preference selection, and committee assignments. All conference data is not only viewable and editable in the online user interface, but is also automatically sorted and written into downloadable Excel spreadsheets that are updated real-time, providing conference organizers with an easy and efficient way to keep track of their registrations.
